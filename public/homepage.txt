### The solution: Surplus Interconnection Service (SIS)

**Surplus Interconnection** lets a *new* resource share an *existing* generator’s unused interconnection capability **without increasing the site’s maximum export**—bypassing the main queue when upgrades aren’t triggered.

* **Federal basis.** FERC **Order 845** (and 845‑A) established SIS across pro‑forma LGIP/LGIA, opening a compliant pathway to use **unused interconnection service** at existing POIs. ([Federal Energy Regulatory Commission][4])
* **PJM (Pennsylvania’s PJM footprint).** In **Feb 2025**, FERC **approved PJM proposals** to **streamline SIS** and a parallel **Reliability Resource Initiative** (RRI) to move shovel‑ready projects faster. PJM confirms the less‑restrictive SIS approach is intended to use existing system capability **without additional network upgrades**. ([PJM Inside Lines][5])
* **PJM (Pennsylvania’s footprint).** In **Feb 2025**, FERC **approved PJM proposals** to **streamline SIS** and a parallel **Reliability Resource Initiative** (RRI) to move shovel‑ready projects faster. ## What is Surplus Interconnection?

**Surplus Interconnection Service (SIS)** lets a new resource inject power using the existing interconnection of an operating generator, as long as the total output doesn’t exceed studied limits. FERC Order **845/845-A** established this pathway; MISO and PJM have SIS processes in place. ([Federal Energy Regulatory Commission][7])

* **MISO**: SIS is codified in Attachment X/BPM-015; 2025 updates increased study deposits and clarified the request path. ([MISO Energy][8])
* **PJM**: FERC approved 2025 reforms expanding SIS eligibility and removing several prior restrictions to speed near-term additions. ([Utility Dive][9])
